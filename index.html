What is Rhetoric and Composition?

Rhetoric and Composition is at the intersection of research, technical communication, creative writing, journalism, and literary analysis. It is a multidisciplinary study that is always concerned with speaker and audience, intent and impact, and context of messages.

Communications scholar, Thomas Farrell, calls rhetoric “the art of making things matter.” Rhetoric is studied with composition and communication because those are the ways rhetoric takes its form. For simplicity’s sake, composition and communication means writing and speaking. But I like to complicate things. So when I tend to ask how different languages, technologies, and orientations to the world change how my writing will land.

I use rhetoric and composition to make things matter. What matters to you?

Copy

I wrote, co-wrote, and revised the following writing samples for various organizations. Additional samples are available upon request.

  • UTHSCSA Writing Lab Brochure : The Writing Lab director requested a brochure containing each section, as they appear, to advertise the lab's services to student users and educate faculty on our support services. I co-authored the brochure's language with one other consultant. However, I was the sole author of the organization's mission statement; I researched other writing labs' mission statements prior to composing our own. Upon the brochure's completion, I created the brochure mockup using a template from Figma Community.
  • Does JoeLister get product descriptions from Amazon? Does that violate copyright? : A JoeLister.com manager requested technical editing for 39 customer support articles. This is one sample that I edited for formatting consistency and procedural clarity, while maintaining the brand's voice. JoeLister does not have an in-house style guide.
  • Letter to Philanthropy Donors : The Styrochrome Creative Consultancy CEO requested an email invititation for business partners to donate to a charity fundraiser. I researched the charity that the CEO had selected and drafted the email persuading the audience to participate.
  • Letter to AU Board Members : A student organization at the University of Texas at Austin requested revisions on a letter expressing their disapproval over the governing board's recent decision. I consulted the writers on the letter's rhetoric and recommended revising their presentation of evidence and justification of involvement. I also made recommendations for local, sentence-level errors.

Research

The following four writing samples are visual abstracts of academic essays. The full manuscripts are available upon request.

  • Computing the Poetic and the Personal with Linguistic - Sentiment Analysis : A study using computational methods to conduct a textual analysis on the emotional appeals of writing for different audiences. I presented this study as a poster at a local conference.
  • En Vaut Deux: Liminality in Graduate School : A personal narrative discussing the limitations of nostaglia, written for the "Affect and Emotions" seminar course. I am preparing this essay for publication in Constellations, an online cultural rhetorics journal.
  • Grade Writing as a Process, not a Product : An essay discussing best practices when teaching with AI in the classroom, co-authored with Spencer Roach. The 2024 Conference on College Composition and Communication accepted this essay for presentation.
  • Pain as Pathos in the Absence of Pride : An essay applying Aristotle's pathos to a contemporary context, written for the "Affect and Emotions" seminar course.
